Genshin Impact — Vietnamese Localization

Brought an open‑world fantasy title to Vietnamese players with day‑one language support. Work covered dialogue adaptation, in‑game text & UI localization, cultural nuance alignment, and linguistic QA. Supported global launch (Patch 1.0 — Sept 2020) and major expansion Inazuma (2.0 — Jul 2021).

Scope Quests, UI, items, artifacts, dialogue
Pipelines EN master to VI (ZH consulted)
Delivery 1.0 launch to 2.2 expansion

Role

  • Translate & adapt EN/ZH source into natural Vietnamese for quests, UI, system strings, items, and artifacts.
  • Maintain terminology consistency via glossaries (Vision, Archon, Fatui, weapons, artifact sets).
  • Align tone for lore‑heavy, poetic dialogue while preserving clarity.
  • Perform linguistic QA: diacritics, line‑breaks, UI clipping, font fallback.
  • Collaborate with multi‑lingual teams; resolve edge cases with EN master and ZH context.
  • Incorporate community feedback to refine key terms post‑release.

Process

  1. 1 Source analysis & terminology planning (with EN master + ZH references when nuance matters).
  2. 2 Translation & adaptation with tone control (poetic yet readable in Vietnamese).
  3. 3 Glossary & consistency pass across items, weapons, artifacts, and UI.
  4. 4 Linguistic QA inside the client: diacritics, line‑wrapping, character widths, clipping.
  5. 5 Release & iterate with community feedback for term refinements.

Impact

  • Supported day‑one Vietnamese localization for global launch (Patch 1.0).
  • Delivered content for Inazuma (Patch 2.0), including Archon Quests and new systems.
  • Improved readability & immersion through tone alignment and consistent terminology.
  • Reduced UI issues by addressing diacritics, wrapping, and font fallback during QA.

Linguistic QA

  • Diacritics & font fallback coverage for Vietnamese glyphs.
  • Line‑wrap & truncation checks across UI states.
  • Width balancing for headers, buttons, and labels.
  • Edge cases: numbers + units, quotation marks, ellipsis, dash usage.

Lessons

  • Use EN master as the pivot, consult ZH for nuance conflicts to avoid semantic drift.
  • Glossary hygiene is compound interest — enforce early to minimize rework post‑release.
  • Design for line length: Vietnamese tends to be longer; budget space in UI.
